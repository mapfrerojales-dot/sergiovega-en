Look.

A few months ago I was reading Trustpilot reviews of Spanish electricity providers.

One stuck with me:

"I've filed 4 complaints and they don't respond."

It was from a customer who had been paying a charge called "Protection 360" on his bill for 2 years. €4.99/month. €119 wasted. A charge he had never agreed to. And when he tried to cancel it, no one picked up the phone.

I scrolled to another review. Same problem.

Another. And another. There were hundreds.

I thought it was an Endesa thing. I checked Iberdrola. Naturgy. Holaluz. The same pattern across all of them, with similar hidden charges.

I work at the Mapfre insurance office in Rojales. For years I've been reviewing contracts and I know the pattern: they slip an additional service into a phone call you don't remember, charge you for years, and when you try to cancel, no one answers.

In insurance, we know how to deactivate it.

I started looking at electricity bills of people close to me. Almost all had hidden charges. Maintenance plans never signed up for. Insurance policies that protected nothing. Things the customer accepted without realising — and kept paying for years.

And here's the thing about expats specifically: most of you have no idea this is happening, because the bill is in Spanish, the call centre is in Spanish, and nobody explains anything in plain English. Even if you suspect something is off, you don't have the tools to prove it.

That's why I now also audit electricity bills.

What everyone asks.

What if I don't save anything?

I tell you and we don't touch anything. No fees. No commitment. The audit is always free.

Will I lose electricity when switching?

No. Switching providers in Spain is like switching mobile providers: same cable, same coverage, different company billing you. Zero outages. Not a single second.

Are there any tie-ins or contracts?

None with me. If the provider has any, I'll tell you upfront — and you decide.

Is this a scam?

Your bill contains public data only (CUPS code, provider name, contracted power). I don't ask for your bank details to audit it. Any change is authorized by YOU with your signature. Nothing happens without your explicit approval.

If it's free, where's the catch?

No catch. The audit is always free — you never pay me. When you switch providers, the new one pays me a small commission for bringing you as a customer. And if there's no real saving for you, we don't switch and nobody pays anything.

I'm only here a few months a year. Does this still apply?

Even more so. Empty property = you keep paying the fixed power charge regardless. Most second homes are massively over-contracted on power. Reducing this alone often saves €200-300/year — money you're throwing away on a bill you barely use.

What about gas and solar panels?

Both. If you have natural gas, I audit it together with electricity. If you have solar panels, even more important: surplus compensation rates vary massively across providers (from €0.02/kWh to €0.10/kWh — five times the difference). Most expats with solar are losing money without knowing it.
